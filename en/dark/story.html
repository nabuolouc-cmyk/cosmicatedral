Genesis

The metamorphosis
of silence.

The belly of the rock

Many years ago, the turmoil of the world grew too heavy. Lucien withdrew behind the rock to seek what could no longer be heard on the surface.

In the absolute silence of a cave, without clocks or landmarks, he began an intimate conversation with silence. It was there, in that fertile twilight wrapped in a divine sun, that Coulouban was born. An isolated retreat where the instrument was no longer an object, but an extension of his soul—the very one with which he learns to love, day after day.

The necessity of sound

In three days, held in the arms of his first accordion, he opened a breach in space-time. The span of learning was swept away by the necessity of breath.

Since that day, he no longer plays music in the classical sense: he lets it pass through him. He becomes the bridge between the silence of the cave and the chaos of the modern world, turning the accordion into a diffuser of harmonic frequencies.

An invisible orchestra

What he hears in the secret of his mind is a mathematical symmetry, a language that words alone sometimes struggle to hold. This is where the rest of his work begins.

If he does not play it, he writes it. If he does not write it, he paints it.
